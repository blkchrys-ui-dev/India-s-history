India's Encyclopedia


Red Fort

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) is a historic Mughal fort in Delhi, India, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century as the primary residence of the Mughal emperors and the hub of Mughal power.Constructed from red sandstone and a mosque.

Bhandara Fort

Bhadra Fort in Ahmedabad is a 15th-century citadel built by the city's founder, Ahmed Shah, The fort complex includes the iconic Teen Darwaja (Triple Gateway), a Bhadrakali Temple, and former palace structures, all located within the historical city walls.

Turkish Gate

The Rumi Darwaza, in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India, is a gateway which was built by Nawab Asaf-Ud-Daula in 1784. It is an example of Awadhi architecture. The Rumi Darwaza is sixty feet tall and was modeled after the Sublime Porte in Istanbul.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers. Hawa Mahal also known as the “palace of winds“ was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.
